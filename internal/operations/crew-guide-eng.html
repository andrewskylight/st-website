Tour Guide

On any given tour, a crew member may fill several roles — captain, deckhand, guide, or some combination. This document addresses the guide role specifically. It is not a complete description of all crew duties.

List of Responsibilities

  • Check in guests
  • Entertain clients
  • Ensure client safety

Notes on conduct:

  • No drinking or smoking weed while on duty
  • Give attention to all guests equally — do not focus only on young females

Major Events During a Tour

  • Check-in (9:00am)
  • Departure (9:30am)
    • 9:45am at the latest, provided the guests called and confirmed they are on their way and running late due to circumstances beyond their control
  • Intro (shortly after departure)
  • Stops 1 & 2, Lunch (around noon)
  • Pig Beach (around 1pm)
  • Outro
  • Guest drop-off (2:55pm – 3:05pm)

9:00am – Arrive at Montague Departure Point

View on Google Maps

List of items needed:

  • Waivers & pen
  • Guest list (internal application or a simple list of guests)

Interaction with a guest (major points):

  • Check-in
  • Waiver
  • Wait for the boat

Sample interaction:

"Hi, are you here for a tour?"

"Which company are you with — Phoenix or Sail Tomorrow?"

[Check people off on the list / app.]

"Please, sign a waiver."

[Once the waiver is signed]

"Alright, just wait in this area. The boat will arrive around 9:15am. I'll gather everyone when it's ready for boarding."

Note: Some guests may become anxious looking for the vessel or worry they were scammed if the boat appears smaller than expected. Informing them that the main boat arrives later usually resolves these concerns.

9:30am – Depart Montague (by 9:45am the Latest)

  • Keep guests on the concrete portion of the dock.
  • Guests who wander onto the wooden portion should be brought back.
  • Once the boat is ready, send guests aboard.
  • The crew will collect shoes (no shoes on the boat); no need to inform guests in advance.
  • Wait for latecomers, then proceed to the vessel.
  • Encourage guests to spread out around the boat — most will crowd near the entry point.
  • Help with undocking if needed.

You are successful when:

  • Guests are on board or marked as no-shows.
  • Waivers are signed.
  • The vessel is undocked and underway.

Briefing / Intro

Within 15 minutes of departure, host a safety/intro briefing.

Start at the stern/back of the boat, then follow up with guests at the front. The captain will slow the boat when you begin.

Intro points

  • Welcome.
  • Three stops: turtles, snorkeling, Pig Beach.
  • Pig Beach: additional charge of $30.
  • Washroom: tissue and garbage go in the bin; no garbage into the bowl.
  • Safety: use life-jackets and lifelines; always hold on. Know the location of safety equipment.
  • Food allergies/restrictions.
  • WiFi: Phoenix777, Alldayfun.
  • Bar is open.

Note: A crew member with a phone will stand beside you to call out the next point.

Intro text (for reference — no need to memorize):

Good morning and welcome aboard Phoenix 777.
My name is Andrew and I'll be your guide today.
Also working for you today is bartender Ray & Carlos, chef Cristian, and our captain Travis.

We have three stops planned.

Our first stop is snorkeling with turtles.

[woohoo, ey — lift up the energy of the group]

Our second stop is snorkeling at the reef.

[ey — lift up the energy again]

This is also where we serve lunch.
Are there any vegans, vegetarians, or any food allergies we need to be aware of?

[Give people a moment to process. Point your hand in three different areas on the boat and repeat the question. You are done when people either said "no" or shook their head "no" — or they gave you the details.]

Our third stop is Pig Beach.

Now Pig Beach is a separate company and they do charge an additional fee of $30 for admission to the island.

Washrooms? Located downstairs — ask crew for directions. Do not throw tissue or garbage in the bowl as it will clog the system. Use the garbage bin in front of you. When finished, use the hand pump. Garbage belongs in the garbage bin.

Safety: life-jackets are at the back of the boat. Crew can assist with putting them on. Always hold onto something when moving around.

Wi-Fi: Access point is Phoenix777, and the password is Alldayfun, capital "A".
[Assist with WiFi requests as needed.]

Do you guys have any questions?

[Usually, people shake their head no.]

Good, because the only question on your mind should be... is the bar open?

[ey — raise up the energy]

Now, the bar is open. Stay seated, no need to move around, the crew will come and take care of you.

(This is especially important if the seas are on the rough side.)

Switch into bartender mode...

You are successful with intro when you emphasize:

  1. Pig Beach is an additional $30 charge
  2. Garbage goes into the garbage bin, and no garbage goes into the toilet bowl
  3. To hold onto something when the boat is moving

Bartender

Goal: serve drinks to guests.

Ensure guests don't wander around the boat, especially early in the tour.

You are successful when:

  • Drinks are prepared and ready.
  • All guests have been asked or served.

[Catch a 10-minute break before the first stop]

Stop 1: Turtle Sighting

(Green Key or Lower Harbour; depending on the wind direction and speed)

Goals:

  • Ensure the boat is anchored safely.
  • Ensure most guests see the turtles from the boat.
  • Assist with fins and snorkels.
  • Guide guests in the water toward turtle sightings.
  • Ensure guest safety: help with equipment, life-jackets, and instruct guests not to walk in fins. Put on fins when guests are ready to enter the water.
  • Prevent snorkeling equipment damage: if someone wants to jump into the water from the boat, they can do so, but without equipment. You can throw them equipment later.

Sample intro text (for inspiration):

First stop:

Alright everyone, this is our first stop. This is where we'll be snorkeling with the turtles. Some people will see turtles from the boat. And we also encourage everyone to go into the water and chase them around.

Crew help with turtles:

The turtles move fast. We'll do our best to assist you in finding them. If a crew member on the boat sees a turtle, they will point to it. I will also go into the water, and if you see my hand up, that means I'm near a turtle. So come closer.

Gear:

Fins, life-jackets, and snorkels are at the back of the boat. The area is large and open, so be sure to get fins.

Getting into the water:

The stairs to get into the water are at the back of the boat as well. This is the easiest way to get into the water.

For those who are more adventurous... We will also open this area (point to the bow of the boat). Those of you who want to jump into the water from here instead of using the stairs, make sure you do that without your equipment on. Either hold on to it in your hands, or have someone else throw it to you.

You are successful when:

  • Guests saw turtles from the boat (ideally also in the water).
  • You attended to guests' needs throughout the stop.
  • The boat departs safely and on time.

Next: Snorkeling at the reef (captain to choose the location)

[Between stops: interact with guests, bar duties]

Stop 2: Snorkeling

(Rose Island or Athol Island; depending on the first stop and wind conditions)

Goals:

  • Ensure the boat is anchored safely.
  • Ensure guests are safe: assist with equipment, life-jackets, instruct not to walk in fins (and put them on when they are ready to go into the water).
  • Prevent snorkeling equipment damage: if someone wants to jump into the water from the boat, they can do so, but without equipment. You can throw them equipment later.

Sample intro text (for inspiration):

Alright guys, this is our second stop. This is where we snorkel with the fish. Please head to the back of the boat to get into the water. If you decide to jump into the water, please do so without your equipment. We will throw it to you later.

IMPORTANT: the reef is that way. (point to the reef)

Take some bread from the kitchen (ask bartenders for help if necessary). Go into the water and spread the bread en-route to the reef. Go around the reef while spreading small pieces of bread to attract the fish. The fish will start moving after you. Swim towards the largest group of guests in the water. Spread some bread there.

You are successful when:

  • You spread the bread in the water to attract the fish.
  • You paid attention to guests' needs throughout the stop.
  • The boat departs safely and on time (captain will know the schedule).

Lunch (around noon)

Lunch is served at the second stop, once most people are out of the water.

Goals:

  • Ensure guests are offered food.
  • Ensure guests' safety (other crew will cover hazard areas).
  • Keep the vessel reasonably clean after meals — nothing loose on the floor or tables (does not need to be spotless).

Sample intro text (for inspiration):

"Alright guys, lunch is ready. It's self-served. Head down inside and get yourself something yummy."

You are successful when:

  • Most guests are eating.
  • Vegetarians, vegans, and others with special diets have received their food.
  • Assist the crew with collecting used plates and cleanup.

Note: Crew will let you know about your own lunch.

Next: Prepare for Pig Beach

Stop 3: Pig Beach (around 1:20pm; sometimes 1:00pm)

The final stop is Pig Beach. By default, we visit Pig Lovers Beach, as no dinghy is required.

In some other places, we may need to anchor and use a dinghy to transport guests to the beach. The crew can handle the details of that scenario.

Goals:

  • Be ready for Pig Beach before arriving at the dock (see below).
  • Ensure all guests have paid for the interaction ($30).
  • Wristbands are on.
  • Pick up all guests from the beach when finished.

Sample intro text (for inspiration):

Alright everyone, we're heading to Pig Beach. Let me see a show of hands — who is doing the pigs?

[Go around the boat and pitch the interaction individually to small groups; handle payments and/or questions.]

Notes:

  • If at any reasonable time the guest changes their mind and decides to do the interaction, accommodate them (creatively solving any challenges if they arise).
  • One crew member will join the guests on the beach and ensure they are looked after (usually the guide, meaning you). Other staff will remain on the boat with guests who prefer to stay on board. If for whatever reason the guide stays on the boat, engage the guests who remain on the boat if they look bored or have nothing to do. If they want to be left alone, leave them alone.
  • A crew member trained on card payments will handle card payments. The captain will handle paying Pig Beach.

You are successful when:

  • Guests who want the interaction have paid and received wristbands.
  • Guests are escorted to the interaction area and Pig Beach staff handle them.
  • After the interaction, all guests are picked up from the island.

Closure / Outro

Goals:

  • Thank guests for joining.
  • Invite tips and reviews.
  • (Optional) Handle birthday celebrations.

After guests are picked up and the vessel has cleared the reef, the captain will slow the boat for the outro. Follow the same pattern: start at the stern/back, then repeat at the bow/front.

Sample outro text (for inspiration):

Alright, everyone, we are about 15 minutes away from the dock, so if you need to call your taxi, now is a good time to do so.

(disappointment sounds: ooh ahh)

BUT before you leave, there are two things we ask you to do.
First [pause], you must enjoy the rest of your vacation.

(woohoo)

Next, please leave a good review on the site you booked on.
Tips are not mandatory, but they are much appreciated.
On behalf of the crew and management staff, we thank you for choosing us today, and we hope to see you again sometime in the future.
Once again, we are 15 minutes away from the dock.

Should a guest ask you to call a taxi for them, you can either call a taxi for them or let them know that there are usually taxis waiting at the parking lot.

You are successful when:

  • You thanked guests for coming.
  • You invited them to leave a review and give tips.

Drop-off (2:55pm – 3:05pm)

Goals:

  • Ensure the vessel is docked safely.
  • Ensure guests disembark safely onto the dock.
  • Divide tips.

You are successful when:

  • Guests have left safely.
  • You leave with your tips.
